Section IV

Full-back evolution

Forty years, three full-backs. The position has changed more than any other on the pitch — from a narrow defensive marker to an attacker who happens to start in the back line.

Three eras define the role:

1980s. The traditional full-back. Stays narrow; marks the winger; rarely crosses the halfway line. Examples: Dennis Irwin, Gary Stevens.

2000s. The overlapping full-back. Pushes high and wide past the winger to provide width and crosses. Examples: Roberto Carlos, Cafu, Danny Alves.

2020s. Two new roles: the inverted full-back (tucks into midfield to create central overloads) and the wing-back (essentially a winger who starts in defence). Examples: João Cancelo, Achraf Hakimi, Trent Alexander-Arnold.

Cancelo vs. Alexander-Arnold

Two opposite answers to the same tactical question. Cancelo (Manchester City 2022) inverted into midfield, leaving the wing for the winger. Alexander-Arnold (Liverpool 2022) pushed into the half-space as a creative passer. Both are full-backs. Neither defends like one.
