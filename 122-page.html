The false 9

The false 9 is a striker who drops off the back line, not into a goal-side position. The role's purpose is to drag a centre-back out of the back four, opening the channel for a winger to run in behind.

Guardiola's Barcelona perfected it with Messi in 2009–11; Italy's 2012 Euros used it with Cassano. Modern Liverpool uses it with Firmino (2018–22), as did Germany's national team.

Why the false 9 works

A traditional striker pins a CB. A false 9 pulls him away from goal and into midfield. Once out, the centre-backs can't cover the central channel — and wingers exploit it.

Modern example

In 2024/25 Ange Postecoglou's Spurs use Son Heung-min as a false 9 with two inverted wide forwards. The effect: defenders don't know who to mark. Postecoglou calls it "creating chaos in the box."
