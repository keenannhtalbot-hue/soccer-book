Ukrainian football

Ukraine's top flight has been disrupted by the 2022 Russian invasion — but Shakhtar and Dynamo Kyiv remain European powers in exile.

Shakhtar Donetsk

Founded 1936. Originally based in Donetsk, now playing in Lviv (temporarily) due to the war. 13 Ukrainian Premier League titles, 1 UEFA Cup (2009). Famous for Brazilian imports: Fernandinho, Willian, Srna.

Dynamo Kyiv

Founded 1927. Plays at the Valeriy Lobanovskyi Stadium (16,870). 16 Ukrainian Premier League titles — the country's record. Famous alumni: Andriy Shevchenko, Oleh Blokhin, Oleg Luzhny.

Football in wartime

After the 2022 invasion, Shakhtar relocated to Lviv, then toured Europe playing "home" matches across multiple countries. Dynamo played Champions League home matches in Poland. The league has continued in spite of everything.

Shevchenko legacy

Andriy Shevchenko (Dynamo Kyiv, AC Milan, Chelsea) won the 2004 Ballon d'Or. He later became Ukraine's national team manager. The greatest Ukrainian footballer of all time.
