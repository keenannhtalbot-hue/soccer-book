Section XII

Underdog stories

The romance of football lives in the underdog stories. Two clubs proving that anything is possible.

Wrexham AFC

Founded 1864. Plays at Racecourse Ground (10,500). The oldest football club in Wales, owned since 2020 by Hollywood stars Rob McElhenney and Ryan Reynolds. Promoted from the National League (5th tier) to League One (3rd tier) in three consecutive seasons (2023, 2024, 2025).

Ipswich Town

Founded 1878. Plays at Portman Road (30,311). Back in the Premier League (2024/25) after 22 years in lower divisions. Manager Kieran McKenna led back-to-back promotions from League One.

Wrexham's documentary

Welcome to Wrexham (FX, 2022) brought the club's story to a global audience. Wrexham's shirt sales, attendance, and brand recognition increased 600% since the documentary launched.

Tractor Boys

Ipswich earned their nickname in the 1960s when local farmers used to drive tractors to away games. The Ipswich fanbase remained loyal through 22 years outside the Premier League — until promotion finally arrived.
