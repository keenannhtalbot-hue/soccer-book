The 5-3-2 — parked bus

Five defenders, three midfielders, two strikers. The 5-3-2 is football’s emergency shape — played for a half, a quarter, or a full match by a team protecting a lead against a superior opponent. The “parked bus”.

José Mourinho named it: “if you have 11 men behind the ball, what is the point of having the ball?” The 5-3-2 minimises central space — the wing-backs tuck in to make a five, the three centre-backs mark the striker, the three midfielders screen the box.

When the equaliser comes, it usually comes from a set piece — because open play is impossible. The 5-3-2 concedes corners and crosses. Inter 2010 (Mourinho) won the Champions League playing it in the knockout rounds.

Hall of fame

Inter 2010 (Mourinho’s treble), Greece 2004 (Rehhagel’s Euro win), Switzerland 2010 (held Spain 1–0 in the World Cup opener). Defensive art.

When to play it

Only as a reaction: a half-time tactical switch, a red-card response, or a European tie against a much stronger opponent. As a default shape, the 5-3-2 concedes too many shots over 90 minutes.

The 5-3-2
5 – 3 – 2

Three centre-backs plus two wing-backs in a defensive five, a midfield three, and two strikers up top. The formation of the underdog defending a one-goal lead in the 89th minute — the "parked bus".
