Press-resistant midfielder

The press-resistant midfielder is the antidote to the high press. They receive and turn under pressure, keep the ball when a team of athletes is trying to win it back in 6 seconds.

This role is now treated as a separate skill. Coaches recruit for it specifically: Rodri, Pedri, Jorginho and Busquets all share the trait. The metric to look for: passes completed under pressure/90.

What makes a midfielder press-resistant

Body shape. Side-on, opens the whole pitch.

First touch. Away from pressure, into space.

Scanning. Looks for the press shape before receiving.

Composure. Holds the ball for an extra beat; pass on the half-volley.

Modern reference

Rodri is top 1% in Europe for passes completed under pressure. Barcelona's Pedri holds the ball for 2.1 seconds on average under pressure — the longest of any midfielder in Europe's top 5 leagues.

Stat
