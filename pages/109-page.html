IX · THE PLAYERS

Fullback · fundamentals

The full-back is football's most-changed role of the last 20 years. Once a pure defender, the modern FB is a wide midfielder who also defends; some invert into central midfield, some act as auxiliary wingers.

Modern physical profile

Top FBs run 10–12 km per match — the highest of any outfield player. They cover the touchline, sprint back 30+ times per game, and complete 2–3 take-ons. Stamina, not athleticism, is the trait that defines a top FB.

What FBs have to do

1v1 defend on the touchline · track overlapping runs · cross with the outside of the foot · recover into a back four after every attack · cover the half-space when the winger inverts.
