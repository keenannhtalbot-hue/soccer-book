Section X · Tactical moves & set plays

Inswinging corners

An inswinger bends toward the goal. From the right corner, a left-footed taker usually curls the ball in; from the left, a right-footer does the same.

The curve makes the goalkeeper defend both the flight and the crowd. Attackers should run across the six-yard box, not wait underneath the dropping ball.

The near-post flash

  • Runner starts outside the near-post defender’s eyeline.
  • A screen or decoy run drags the marker toward the front zone.
  • The delivery is whipped into the corridor between the post and the penalty spot.
  • A second attacker attacks the goalkeeper’s rebound zone.

Goalkeeper problem

An inswinger that clears the first defender may still be dangerous. The keeper must decide whether to attack a moving ball through bodies or hold the line and trust the defence.
