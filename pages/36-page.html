Section IV

The 3-5-2 — wing-back era

Three centre-backs for width in defence, two wing-backs who cover the entire touchline, a midfield three, and two strikers. The 3-5-2 is the formation of the Italian school — and, lately, of Antonio Conte.

The trade is stark: the wing-backs give you two extra players in midfield, but leave huge channels behind them. If the wing-backs are caught high, the outside centre-backs are exposed one-on-one against opposing wingers.

Three at the back demands three centre-backs who can play — one ball-playing sweeper, two aggressive markers. The shape has come back in cycles: Bergomi-Baresi-Costacurta for Milan, Bonucci-Chiellini-Barzagli for Juventus and Italy.

Hall of fame

Juventus 2015–17, Italy 1990 (Sacco), Chelsea 2016–17 (Conte), Inter 2020–21 (Conte). One shape, multiple success stories.

The wing-back engine

In a 3-5-2, the wing-back covers more ground than any other player on the pitch — typically 11–12 km per match, the highest of any outfield role. Jardel, Hakimi, Perisic — all are essentially wingers wearing defensive jerseys.
