IX · THE PLAYERS

The number 8 · role deconstructed

The number 8 is football's most-quietly-evolved position. Once the team's box-to-box runner, the modern 8 has split into three sub-types — each with a different vertical lane.

Three sub-8s

The box-to-box 8 — covers full pitch. Defends, attacks, never stops. (Kanté, Valverde.)

The half-space 8 (mezzala). Drifts wide to create, picks up the half-spaces. (Barella, Musiala.)

The double-pivot 8. Plays next to the 6 in a 4-2-3-1; defends more than attacks. (Kroos, Toni Kroos at Real Madrid 2014–24.)

Why this matters

Coaches recruit by sub-type. Declan Rice is a box-to-box; Pedri is a half-space; Toni Kroos was a pivot-8. Same shirt number, very different jobs.
