The inside forward

The inside forward is a winger who tucks into the half-space. They start on the touchline but attack inside the channel. The role was named after Hurst, Greaves and the 1966 World Cup — and now describes Saka, Salah, Vinícius Júnior.

The point: a winger running away from the touchline (towards the far post) is hard to defend. The FB has to choose between tracking the runner or holding the line.

Tactical birth

Brazil used the inside forward through Garrincha and Pelé. Hungary's 1954 final team ran a similar tactic against Brazil. The role's tactical birth is credited to the Hungarian coach Gusztáv Sebes.

Modern benchmark

Vinícius Júnior at Real Madrid 2021–24: starts wide, attacks the channel, scores like a 9. Mohamed Salah at Liverpool: same brief, but inside-out.

Stat

Vinícius Júnior scored 24 goals in 2023/24 La Liga — the most by a winger that season. From an inside-forward role.
