Section XII

Dutch football

The Eredivisie is the world's most prolific talent factory. Ajax, PSV, and Feyenoord are the historic three.

Feyenoord

Founded 1908. Plays at De Kuip (47,500). 16 Eredivisie titles. Rotterdam's working-class pride. Won the European Cup in 1970 with the legendary Ove Kindvall goal.

PSV Eindhoven

Founded 1913. Plays at Philips Stadion (35,000). 24 Eredivisie titles — the most. Funded by Philips Electronics. Won the European Cup in 1988 under Guus Hiddink.

The De Kuip

Feyenoord's stadium — "the Tub" — was once the most intimidating away day in Europe. Visiting players regularly reported the stadium vibrating under the bouncing crowd.
