IX · THE PLAYERS

No-nonsense CB

The no-nonsense centre-back is the footballer's anti-couture: a specialist in duels, headers, and tackles. They mark tight, clear first-time, and rarely progress with the ball — because they don't need to.

The archetype dominated English football for 25 years and remains alive in many defensive systems. In a three-at-the-back shape they are essential; in a four, they pair best with a ball-playing partner.

Five signature traits

1. Standing tackle — shoulder into the chest, foot on the ball.
2. Aerial dominance — most clearances headed, not kicked.
3. Aggression — first to the duels, last to complain.
4. Vertical passing — five-yard options, then out.
5. 90-minute concentration — the tackle win rate almost never dips.

Modern examples

Marquinhos at PSG, Kalidou Koulibaly at Chelsea 2022–24, Dan Burn at Newcastle. Aged well: their tackling numbers rarely fall even when legs slow — because the position is technical, not just physical.
