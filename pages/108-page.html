CB in build-up

Build-up has redrawn the centre-back's job description. Where the 1990s CB was a long-ball launcher, the 2020s CB is a first attacking option — the deepest midfielder on the team.

The four build-up phases

1. GK to CB. The third-man restart. The CB receives with a wide-open body.

2. CB split wide. To the FB, to open the pitch's width.

3. CB carries. Drives 5–10 m past the first press line — a £30m signing used to cost the team this now-routine carry.

4. CB line-breaker. The final ball into the 8 or the half-space 10.

Why CB build-up matters

A team that can't progress past the first press gives the ball back within 8 seconds of winning it. Build-out CBs effectively shift the line of confrontation 10 m up the pitch and add 0.15 expected goals per game.
