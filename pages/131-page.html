Corners: the restart with a plan

A DEAD BALL, A LIVE ADVANTAGE

A corner is a free delivery into the most crowded part of the pitch. The best routines do not rely on luck: they create a starting picture, move defenders, and attack the space that opens.

The five questions

  • Who delivers? In-swing, outswing, or short option?
  • Who screens? Can a legal block give the first runner a clean lane?
  • Where is the first contact? Near post, penalty spot, back post, or edge?
  • Who protects the counter? Set a rest defence before the kick.
  • What happens next? Rehearse second balls, not only the first header.

The crowded-box rule

More bodies do not automatically mean more danger. If five attackers stand in the same lane, one defender can block all five. Stagger starting positions so each runner has a different route.

Basic corner geometry

The corner is a sequence: delivery, movement, first contact, second ball, and counter-press.

Set-piece principle

Change the picture before the ball arrives

A small starting movement can force a defender to turn, pass on a runner, or leave a zone. The delivery then attacks the new picture—not the original one.
