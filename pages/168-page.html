Away day culture

TRAVELLING WITH THE CREST

An away day turns supporters into a travelling community. The destination, route, ticket allocation, and welcome from the host city all become part of the fixture.

Away-day essentials

  • Ticket: away allocations are limited; many clubs use loyalty or ballot systems.
  • Route: trains, coaches, metros, and walking corridors are planned around kick-off.
  • Colours: a scarf or shirt makes identity visible, but local advice matters in tense fixtures.
  • Return: clubs and authorities coordinate dispersal so thousands leave safely.

Reciprocity travels

Fans remember how a host city treats visitors. Shared pubs, respectful welcomes, accessible transport, and clear stewarding can turn a rivalry into a day worth repeating.

The moving stand

An away section is a portable piece of club identity. Its songs and colours are compressed into a smaller block, making the group feel both exposed and unusually close.

AWAY ETIQUETTE

Be a guest and a representative

Follow ticket conditions, respect local residents, avoid blocking exits, and remember that the badge on your shirt is visible long after one argument is forgotten.
