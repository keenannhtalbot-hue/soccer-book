Short corners

TWO AGAINST ONE, BEFORE THE CROSS

A short corner moves the ball away from the flag before the cross. Its first job is not always a shot; it can move the defensive block, create a better crossing angle, or open the edge of the box.

The three-player triangle

  • The taker plays a firm pass to the nearby teammate.
  • A third player runs beyond or under the receiver to create a choice.
  • The receiver can cross, combine again, or carry the ball into the box.
  • A safety player stays behind the ball to stop the counter.

Do not fake the idea

A short routine only works if the defenders believe the cross is still coming. Vary the delivery, the receiver, and the final action so the corner does not become a predictable two-pass signal.

The hidden benefit

Even when the short corner does not beat a defender, it can pull one marker out of the six-yard box and make the next cross more dangerous.
