IX · THE PLAYERS

FB defending

For all the talk of overlap and crosses, the full-back wins games in the defensive transitions. Top FBs cover the touchline, make 3+ tackles per match, and intercept 1.2 passes per 90.

Five defensive tasks

1v1 defending. Side-on stance, one foot on the touchline. Don't give the free cross.
Cover the half-space. When the winger inverts, fill the inside-out channel.
Delay the counter. After losing the ball, the FB is the first line of recovery.
Aerial duels. Wings are high-cross territory — clear, then reset.
Positioning. Always shoulder-check: outside winger, inside 8, behind CB.

Modern reference

Rúben Dias / Cancelo at City, Alphonso Davies at Bayern, Daniel Muñoz at Crystal Palace — the best FBs defend first, then attack.
