IX · THE PLAYERS

The ball-playing CB

The ball-playing centre-back is the cornerstone of possession football: a CB who can pass through the press, switch play, and progress the ball into midfield with a single line-breaking pass.

Guardiola's Barcelona popularized the role; Pep's Manchester City standardized it. The most-coveted metric is progressive passes per 90 — top ball-playing CBs hit 8–12, almost double a defensive CB's ~5.

Line-breaker profile

Sits on the half-turn. Receives with a wide open body, head up before the ball arrives. Plays the pass through the press instead of going around it. They are usually the deeper of a CB pair — the "left CB" in a Guardiola system.

Modern reference

Rúben Dias combined ball-playing calm (88.7% pass accuracy over his first City season) with recovery speed. Ronald Araújo played 200+ progressive passes in 2022/23 yet still ranks top 3% for tackles won. Players no longer choose between passing and defending.

The progressive CB
Stat

In 2023/24, top-5 European league CBs averaged 5.4 progressive passes per 90 — a 35% rise on 2018/19.
