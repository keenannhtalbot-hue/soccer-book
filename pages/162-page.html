Section XI · Fan culture & history

Tifos

CHOREOGRAPHY IN THE STANDS

A tifo is a coordinated stadium display: cards, flags, giant banners, painted sheets, or a three-dimensional scene revealed at a precise moment.

Built before the gates open

  • Sketch: the image must read from the far side of the stadium.
  • Translate: artists turn the sketch into numbered panels or colour blocks.
  • Stage: volunteers place every card, banner, and flag in its seat zone.
  • Reveal: a cue from the capo or announcer turns hundreds of pieces into one picture.

The visual grammar

Strong tifos use high contrast, a simple silhouette, and one idea: a club crest, a historic player, a local symbol, or a message to the rival. Complexity is the enemy of legibility.

The reveal

Tifos are temporary works of art. Their impact comes from scale, timing, and the knowledge that everyone in the section made the image together.

SAFETY FIRST

Paper, paint, and people

Modern displays are planned around fire regulations, steward access, sightlines, and emergency routes. The best choreography is spectacular without closing a safe exit.
