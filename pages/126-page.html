IX · THE PLAYERS

The winger

A winger is a wide attacker who stays close to the touchline. The role is the most-celebrated in football: Cristiano Ronaldo, Lionel Messi, Mohamed Salah all began as wingers.

The modern winger has more tactical variety than ever. They can be traditional (stays wide, crosses) or inverted (cuts inside onto the strong foot). The choice of foot determines everything.

Three winger types

The traditional winger. Stays wide, puts the cross in. Gareth Bale early career.

The inverted winger. Cuts inside, shoots/creates. Arjen Robben.

The inside forward. Plays between full-back and centre-back. Mohamed Salah.

The inverted winger's revolution

Robben's diagonal cut inside onto his left foot became the most-copied move of the 2010s. Saka at Arsenal, Sterling at Man City, Salah at Liverpool all use variants of it.
