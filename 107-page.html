IX · THE PLAYERS

CB partnerships

A centre-back partnership isn't two players added together — it's a single defensive unit. The best pairs complement: ball-playing + no-nonsense, left-foot + right-foot, leader + lieutenant.

Modern systems prefer contrast: one CB progresses the ball, the other covers the runs behind. Both defend. Only one acts as the team's first attacker.

Three partnership types

Balanced. Two similar CBs — Atlético's Giménez + Savić until 2023.
Split. One progresses, one defends — Van Dijk + Konaté at Liverpool.
Leader + sidekick. A vocal organiser and a quieter runner — Ramos + Varane.

The foot match

Traditionally a CB pair needed opposite feet to open the pitch (a right-footed CB on the right covers the touchline; a left-footed CB on the left inverts). Modern systems invert this — both CBs often play inside-foot on the ball, regardless of side.

Stat

CB pairs that have played 200+ matches together concede ~0.12 fewer goals per 90 than the league average.

Most stable: Van Dijk + Matip (Liverpool); Bonucci + Chiellini (Juventus); Ramos + Varane (Real Madrid).
