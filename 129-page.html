IX · THE PLAYERS

The pressing forward

A pressing forward is a striker whose job is to win the ball back from the opposition's defence — before they have even had it for ten seconds. They start the press; the team follows.

The role's two archetypes are the Fox-in-the-Box (Lewandowski, Kane) and the Pressing Fox (Suárez, Firmino). Modern football now expects even pure goalscorers to press — Haaland runs 8+ km per game.

Pressing zones

1. Block the centre-back's vertical pass. Show outside.

2. Force into the wide channel. Trap with the winger.

3. Curve runs to cut the lane. Don't dive in.

4. Press on the back foot of the CB. When the CB receives sideways, press.

Reference: Firmino's press

At Liverpool 2018–22, Roberto Firmino led the press as a false-9 pressing forward. In the 2018/19 Champions League-winning run, Liverpool regained the ball in the opposition third on 38% of possessions — much of it from Firmino's work.
