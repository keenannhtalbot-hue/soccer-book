The 4-1-4-1 — defensive block

A back four, a single defensive midfielder as a shield, a flat line of four ahead of them, and a lone striker. The 4-1-4-1 is the formation for the team without the ball — the deep block that makes space look small.

The aim is to compress play into a 30-metre band in front of the box. The four-bank-mid-four press any pass into that band, the striker closes the centre-backs’ passing lane, and the anchor screens the back four.

The trade-off: it concedes possession. Atlético Madrid under Simeone averaged 43% possession in La Liga with it — but won two league titles, two Europa Leagues, and reached two Champions League finals.

Hall of fame

Atlético Madrid 2013–16 (Simeone), Leicester City 2015–16 (Ranieri), Chelsea 2004–06 (Mourinho’s first Chelsea). The underdog’s shape.

The anchor’s job

The single pivot (the 6) is the most isolated role on the pitch — no partner to share the work. Busquets, Kanté, Casemiro: each is a one-man midfield, screening four defenders and launching the counter from the half-turn.

A back four, a single defensive midfielder (the anchor), a bank of four midfielders, and one striker. The shape of choice for teams playing on the counter — Atlético Madrid under Simeone in particular.
