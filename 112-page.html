IX · THE PLAYERS

Wingback evolution

The wingback is the most pronounced wing-player in modern football: a wide player in a back three who plays in the opposition half for 60% of the game. The role dates from the 1960s (Helenio Herrera's Inter) but reached tactical peak in 2010s/2020s.

Used in 3-5-2, 3-4-2-1, 3-4-1-2 and the morphing 3-4-3, the wingback is now a specialist position. Players like Achraf Hakimi, Robin Gosens and João Cancelo at Manchester City have driven the role.

The wingback's brief

Stay wide, get high. Sprint in behind the opposing winger and put the cross in. Defend one-on-one when the team loses the ball, then sprint back to the wing again. The role is unique in covering 100+ m vertically per possession.

Three-at-the-back revival

Antonio Conte's 3-5-2 at Chelsea in 2016/17 won the Premier League with Victor Moses and Marcos Alonso as wingbacks; the same shape at Inter won Serie A in 2020/21 with Hakimi and Young.
