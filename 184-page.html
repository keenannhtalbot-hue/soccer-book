Women stars

THE GAME HAS MANY CENTRES

Women’s football now has a constellation of stars across roles, leagues, and countries. The point is not one “face of the game” but a wider set of players who make different pathways visible.

What a star can represent

  • Aitana Bonmatí: midfield intelligence, pressing, and the creative rhythm of Barcelona and Spain.
  • Sam Kerr: movement, finishing, and a global audience built across Australia, the NWSL, and England.
  • Alex Morgan: longevity, leadership, and the commercial growth of the United States game.
  • Trinity Rodman: direct attacking play and a new generation comfortable across club, country, and digital media.

Stars are systems, too

Behind every headline player are coaches, analysts, physios, teammates, family, academy staff, and leagues that make a high-level career possible.

Routes to the spotlight

Stars expand the imagination of the game. A young player can see different body types, positions, nationalities, personalities, and careers succeeding on the same stage.

ROLE MODELS

Representation is practical

A visible player can change what a child believes is possible—and what a club believes it should build to keep that child in the game.
