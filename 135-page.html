Near post, back post, edge of box

A complete corner playbook changes the target. Near-post runs attack speed and surprise; back-post runs attack space; the edge of the box attacks the clearance.

TargetBest forMain risk
Near postSpeed, flick-onsFirst defender clears
Back postLate headers, overloadsBall hangs too long
Edge of boxSecond shotsCounter if overcommitted

The rest-defence rule

Keep at least two players behind or around the ball, with another ready to stop the first pass. A corner is an attacking chance—but conceding a three-on-two counter is a tactical own goal.

Coach’s note

Rehearse the second action

A corner routine is not finished when the first header misses. Assign the next three jobs: attack the loose ball, recycle the possession, and protect the transition.
