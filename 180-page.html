Fantasy football

MANAGING THE IMAGINARY SQUAD

Fantasy football turns watching into a second decision game. A supporter selects a squad, chooses a captain, manages a budget, and scores points from real matches.

The basic loop

  • Pick: build within a budget and squad rules.
  • Plan: compare fixtures, minutes, roles, and set-piece duties.
  • Manage: transfer, captain, bench, and use a limited chip or wildcard.
  • Review: compare the real match with the points the game awarded.

A different kind of attention

Fantasy managers watch minutes, substitutions, assists, clean sheets, bonus systems, and fixture runs. It adds tactical literacy—but it can also make a fan care about an individual event more than the result.

From match to points

Fantasy games translate football into a scoring model. The model is useful for engagement, but it is never the whole sport: defensive work, emotion, and context do not fit neatly into points.

FAIR PLAY

Play the game, protect the habit

Set a budget, avoid chasing losses in paid formats, and remember that a fantasy score is entertainment—not a measure of a person’s worth as a supporter.
