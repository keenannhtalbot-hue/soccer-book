IX · THE PLAYERS

Goalkeeper · distribution

Build-out is now the single most-studied GK behaviour. A keeper who can play short under pressure is a free possession for the team, a +0.10 xG opener for the next attack, and the trigger for every high press in football.

The five passes

Modern GKs split distribution into five channels:

1. Short to CB — the safe third-man option.

2. Split to FB — inside the full-back with a one-touch.

3. Half-space 8 — the most common line-breaking option.

4. Switch to far FB — 50+ yards, used to reset the press.

5. Long to ST — bypass-press, second-ball territory.

The Ederson effect

No GK has reshaped distribution like Ederson. In 2017–18 he completed 84% of passes longer than 30 yards — a number that would have been absurd a decade earlier. Pep Guardiola bought him because, in his words, "he plays as an extra midfielder."
