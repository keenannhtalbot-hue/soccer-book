Section XI · Fan culture & history

Ultras culture

THE TERRACE AS ORGANIZER

Ultras are organized supporter groups who turn a stand into a visual, musical, and logistical force. They are not simply “louder fans”: they build rituals around belonging.

What an ultras group does

  • Organizes: banners, flags, drums, coordinated displays, and travel.
  • Funds: choreographies through member dues, collections, and merchandise.
  • Communicates: through leaders, printed flyers, encrypted chats, and terrace signals.
  • Represents: a local identity, neighbourhood, political mood, or club history.

Ultras ≠ hooligans

Ultras culture is a supporter tradition; hooliganism describes organized violence. The two can overlap in some groups, but they are not synonyms. Clubs and police increasingly separate choreography from prohibited items and disorder.

A terrace is a system

The visible display depends on unseen work: collecting materials, assigning sections, rehearsing songs, and moving safely through turnstiles.

TERRACE ETHIC

The group protects the ritual

A good capo does not just start a chant. They watch the whole stand, cue the next section, and leave space for the crowd to answer.
