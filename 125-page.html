IX · THE PLAYERS

Advanced playmaker

The advanced playmaker is the final-third passer: a 10 who, instead of operating between midfield and defence, lives in the opposition's last 20 metres. Their job is to break the last line of defence.

The role is distinct from the classical 10: they don't drop off; they receive to feet between the lines and play vertical, not possession.

Two specialisms

The passer. Splits the back line with a through-ball. Mesut Özil at Real Madrid 2010–13.

The runner. Plays behind the strikers and arrives in the box. Johan Cruyff at Barcelona 1973–78.

The Cruyff turn

No role embodies the advanced playmaker like Johan Cruyff's Barcelona shirt. He played what is now called a "false 9" but functioned as the most advanced midfielder in football. The technique, the position and the role are all named after him.
