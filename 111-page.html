IX · THE PLAYERS

Attacking full-back

The attacking full-back is an auxiliary winger who can also defend. They make 4–6 overlaps per match, complete 1.5–2 crosses from open play, and run 11+ km per game.

The role was pioneered by Cafu and Dani Alves at the turn of the century. It is now the standard for elite European teams. The best attacking FBs score or assist with the regularity of midfielders.

Three sub-types

The crosser. Stays wide, plays the cross first-time — Trent Alexander-Arnold.
The runner. Overlaps in behind, takes the byline — Achraf Hakimi.
The creator. Pulls inside as an extra midfielder, creates with the inside foot — Jordi Alba.

Trent Alexander-Arnold

In 2018/19 he registered 12 Premier League assists — more than any other Liverpool player. Among Europe's top 5 leagues, only five midfielders bettered his creative numbers. He redefined the role.
