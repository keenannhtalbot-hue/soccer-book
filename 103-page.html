IX · THE PLAYERS

Sweeper-keeper

A sweeper-keeper plays 12–25 metres off the goal line, behind a high defensive line. Their job is to read the game one step ahead — intercept through-balls, smother one-on-ones, and act as a last defender when the centre-backs step up to engage.

The role requires two un-GK skills: the speed of a centre-back (sprint tests: 6.0+ m/s) and the courage to leave an empty net. Since the late 2000s, almost every elite GK has been trained as a sweeper.

Reading the through-ball

A sweeper-keeper's signature is interception outside the box. They weigh the run, the pass weight, and the runner's momentum; they sprint off their line and clear first-time. Modern elite keepers record 0.3–0.6 such interventions per game.

Manuel Neuer

The reference model. During Bayern's 2019–20 treble, Neuer completed more touches outside his box (8.1 per match) than any other Bundesliga GK by some distance. Germany's 2014 World Cup win rebuilt football's idea of what a goalkeeper could be.

Modern reference

Alisson completed 62 sweepings in Liverpool's 2019 Champions League-winning run — the most of any keeper that season.
