Future of women's football

THE NEXT 90 MINUTES

The future of women’s football will be decided less by one headline event than by the quality of the everyday system: who can enter, stay, earn, travel, watch, and lead.

Five priorities

  • Pathways: affordable entry, school links, local clubs, and visible routes from youth to senior football.
  • Professional standards: contracts, training time, medical care, safeguarding, and facilities that match the ambition.
  • Calendar design: fewer destructive clashes and more predictable windows for leagues and internationals.
  • Media access: consistent broadcasts, strong storytelling, and highlights that reach casual fans.
  • Voice: players, supporters, coaches, and local communities shaping the sport’s governance.

Growth should be durable

A record crowd is a beginning, not a solution. Durable growth means a supporter can buy a ticket next season, a player can plan a career, and a club can budget for the women’s team as a core part of its identity.

Build the whole pathway

The next era is not only about filling bigger stadiums. It is about making the route to those stadiums reliable for players, supporters, officials, and clubs.

LOOKING AHEAD

The ceiling is still moving

When access, quality, visibility, and leadership improve together, the game does not merely grow—it changes what football can look like for everyone.
