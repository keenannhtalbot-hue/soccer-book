IX · THE PLAYERS

Centre-back · fundamentals

The centre-back is the cheapest position to fill badly and the most expensive position to fill well. A top CB is the spine of any defence: 1v1, in the air, on the ground, on the turn, and now with the ball.

Modern CBs stand 186–195 cm tall, weigh 80–90 kg, and run the 30-metre sprint in under 4.0 seconds. They duel 8–12 times per match and win roughly 65%. Above that figure, they are the team's dominant aerial presence.

Three core skills

1v1 duels. Body shape, side-on stance, knee over the ball.
Aerial duels. Timing the jump — peak jump is at the shoulder height of the contact.
Reading the game. Scanning every 5–7 seconds; knowing what's behind you before the ball arrives.

Two distinct postures

Closed stance (side-on, ball in front) is the safer posture for marking a runner. Open stance (facing your own goal) is better for cutting passing lanes. Top CBs swap postures instinctively.
